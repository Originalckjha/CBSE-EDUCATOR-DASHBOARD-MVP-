# Code of Conduct

## 🤝 Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in the CBSE Educator Platform community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of:

- Age
- Body size
- Disability (visible or invisible)
- Ethnicity
- Sex characteristics
- Gender identity and expression
- Level of experience
- Education
- Socio-economic status
- Nationality
- Personal appearance
- Race
- Religion
- Sexual identity and orientation
- Caste or community

We are committed to providing a safe, respectful, and inclusive environment for all educators, developers, and contributors.

-----

## 🌟 Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

✅ **Respectful Communication**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what’s best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members

✅ **Collaborative Spirit**

- Helping newcomers get started
- Sharing knowledge and resources
- Acknowledging contributions of others
- Working together towards common goals
- Celebrating successes of community members

✅ **Professional Conduct**

- Providing constructive feedback
- Staying on topic in discussions
- Being patient with those learning
- Respecting project maintainers’ decisions
- Following contribution guidelines

✅ **Inclusive Practices**

- Using gender-neutral language
- Being mindful of cultural differences
- Accommodating accessibility needs
- Welcoming diverse perspectives
- Creating opportunities for all voices

### Unacceptable Behaviors

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

❌ **Harassment & Discrimination**

- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, appearance, body size, race, religion, or caste
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Sustained disruption of discussions
- Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

❌ **Disrespectful Communication**

- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing others’ private information (doxxing)
- Spamming or excessive self-promotion
- Using sexualized language or imagery

❌ **Unprofessional Conduct**

- Deliberately derailing conversations
- Making false or misleading claims
- Plagiarizing others’ work
- Violating confidentiality
- Abuse of power or position

❌ **Technical Misconduct**

- Introducing malicious code
- Deliberately breaking the platform
- Violating security policies
- Unauthorized access to systems
- Ignoring security vulnerability reports

-----

## 👨‍⚖️ Enforcement Responsibilities

### Maintainer Responsibilities

Community leaders and project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate

### Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:

- Within all project spaces (GitHub, discussions, chat channels)
- In public spaces when representing the project
- In private communications related to the project
- At project events and meetups

## 📢 Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Primary Contact:**

- Email: conduct@cbseeducator.in
- Response Time: Within 48 hours

**Alternative Contacts:**

- Project Lead: lead@cbseeducator.in
- Community Manager: community@cbseeducator.in

**Anonymous Reporting:**

- Form: https://cbseeducator.in/report-conduct
- All reports can be made anonymously

### What to Include in Your Report

Please provide as much information as possible:

1. **Your Contact Information** (optional for anonymous reports)
1. **Names/Identities** of people involved
1. **Description** of the incident
1. **When and Where** it occurred
1. **Witnesses** (if any)
1. **Context** or background information
1. **Supporting Evidence** (screenshots, links, etc.)
1. **Impact** on you or others
1. **Previous Incidents** (if applicable)
1. **Preferred Outcome** (what would help resolve this)

### Report Template

```markdown
## Incident Report

**Date & Time:** [When did this occur?]
**Location:** [GitHub issue, PR, email, chat, etc.]
**Reporter:** [Your name or "Anonymous"]

**People Involved:**
- Person(s) who violated the CoC:
- Witnesses:

**Description:**
[Detailed description of what happened]

**Evidence:**
[Links, screenshots, or other documentation]

**Impact:**
[How this affected you or others]

**Desired Outcome:**
[What would you like to see happen?]

**Additional Context:**
[Anything else we should know]
```

## 🔍 Investigation Process

### Timeline

1. **Acknowledgment** - Within 48 hours of report
1. **Initial Review** - Within 1 week
1. **Investigation** - 1-2 weeks (varies by complexity)
1. **Decision** - Communicated to all parties
1. **Appeal Period** - 14 days from decision

### Confidentiality

- All reports are kept confidential
- Information shared only on need-to-know basis
- Reporter identity protected (unless waived)
- Investigation details remain private
- Public statements (if needed) protect privacy

### Fair Process

We ensure:

- ✅ Impartial investigation
- ✅ All parties heard
- ✅ Evidence-based decisions
- ✅ Proportionate responses
- ✅ Right to appeal

## ⚖️ Enforcement Actions

### Warning Levels

**Level 1: Verbal Warning**

- **Trigger**: Minor first offense
- **Action**: Private discussion, explanation of violation
- **Record**: Internal note, no public record
- **Duration**: N/A

**Level 2: Written Warning**

- **Trigger**: Repeated minor violations or single moderate violation
- **Action**: Formal written warning, expectations clarified
- **Record**: Documented in moderation log
- **Duration**: 6 months on record

**Level 3: Temporary Ban**

- **Trigger**: Serious violation or pattern of behavior
- **Action**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Duration**: 7-90 days depending on severity
- **Record**: Permanent moderation record
- **Reinstatement**: Requires acknowledgment and commitment to change

**Level 4: Permanent Ban**

- **Trigger**: Severe violations, threats, harassment, or repeated serious offenses
- **Action**: Permanent removal from all community spaces
- **Record**: Permanent ban record
- **Appeal**: Limited appeal process (30 days)

### Immediate Actions

For severe violations, maintainers may:

- Immediately remove offending content
- Temporarily suspend accounts pending investigation
- Block access to community spaces
- Report to appropriate authorities (if illegal activity)

## 📋 Examples & Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Disrespectful Language

**Situation**: A contributor uses dismissive language toward a beginner asking questions.

**Violation**: Disrespectful communication, not welcoming to newcomers

**Response**: Level 1 warning, discussion about creating welcoming environment

**Outcome**: Contributor apologizes, modifies behavior

-----

### Scenario 2: Spam and Self-Promotion

**Situation**: User repeatedly posts unrelated promotional content in discussions.

**Violation**: Spamming, disrupting community discussions

**Response**: Level 2 warning, content removed

**Outcome**: If continued, temporary ban (7 days)

-----

### Scenario 3: Harassment

**Situation**: Individual sends unwelcome personal messages to another contributor.

**Violation**: Harassment, creating unsafe environment

**Response**: Level 3-4 action depending on severity, immediate temporary ban

**Outcome**: Investigation, potential permanent ban

-----

### Scenario 4: Technical Sabotage

**Situation**: Contributor intentionally introduces malicious code.

**Violation**: Technical misconduct, security violation

**Response**: Level 4 immediate permanent ban, security review

**Outcome**: Permanent ban, potential legal action

## 🌈 Creating an Inclusive Environment

### Language Guidelines

**Use inclusive language:**

- ✅ “Hey everyone” or “Hi folks”
- ❌ “Hey guys”
- ✅ “they/them” when gender unknown
- ❌ Assuming gender
- ✅ “the person who wrote this code”
- ❌ “the guy who wrote this”

### Accessibility Practices

**In Documentation:**

- Use clear, simple language
- Provide alt text for images
- Use descriptive link text
- Structure with proper headings
- Include captions for videos

**In Communication:**

- Be patient with non-native English speakers
- Use emojis thoughtfully (may not be accessible)
- Describe images in chat
- Avoid ableist language

### Cultural Sensitivity

Remember:

- Different time zones and working hours
- Cultural holidays and observances
- Varied communication styles
- Language barriers
- Regional differences in expression

## 🎓 For Educators Specifically

### Professional Standards

As educators, we hold ourselves to high standards:

- **Integrity**: Honest representation of qualifications and experience
- **Respect**: Professional interaction with all community members
- **Privacy**: Protecting student and school information
- **Quality**: Sharing accurate, well-researched educational content
- **Safety**: Ensuring resources are age-appropriate and safe

### Sharing Educational Content

When sharing resources:

- ✅ Verify accuracy before sharing
- ✅ Provide proper attribution
- ✅ Respect copyright and licenses
- ✅ Consider cultural sensitivity
- ✅ Indicate age-appropriateness
- ❌ Don’t share personal student information
- ❌ Don’t plagiarize or misrepresent authorship

## 🤔 Questions & Clarifications

### Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What if I’m not sure if something violates the CoC?**
A: When in doubt, report it. We’d rather review a non-issue than miss a real problem.

**Q: Can I report something that happened in the past?**
A: Yes, there’s no time limit on reporting violations.

**Q: What if the violation involves a maintainer?**
A: Report to an alternative contact. External mediators may be involved if needed.

**Q: Will my report really be kept confidential?**
A: Yes, we take confidentiality seriously and share information only as necessary for investigation.

**Q: What if I disagree with an enforcement decision?**
A: You have 14 days to appeal. Contact conduct@cbseeducator.in with your appeal and new information.

**Q: Can I see the moderation log?**
A: Individual records are confidential. Aggregate statistics may be shared quarterly.
